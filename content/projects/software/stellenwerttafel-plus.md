***Stellenwerttafel+*** was an educational application developed by Dynamotivation in early 2019 at age 16. It represented a multi-platform port and enhancement of [Ulrich Kortenkamp](https://kortenkamps.net)'s ["Stellenwerttafel" iPad app](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/place-value-chart/id568750442), designed to facilitate a playful understanding of various number systems for students. Developed as an internal school project, its creation provided significant insights into client-driven development, cross-platform adaptation, and the importance of intellectual property.


{{ big_heading(name="Development") }}

---

Development for *Stellenwerttafel+* took place over a few days in late January and early February 2019. The project originated as a "contract work" assignment for school, undertaken at age 16 in exchange for promissed extra credit.\
The school faced a challenge: A lack of iPads for IT classes, reliance on old Windows 7 PCs, and prohibition of private phone use. This necessitated a solution to bring the interactive "Stellenwerttafel" educational tool to cheaper platforms.

After proofing GameMaker Studio 1.4 as a viable platform for both games and productive apps, it was choosen for both Windows and Android support. This effort addressed the school's immediate needs by extending the live of their old devices. The original "Stellenwerttafel" app, by Ulrich Kortenkamp, served as the foundational inspiration, particularly its interactive approach to teaching number systems through visual manipulation.

Although development was brought to completion after school within a week and the final result was accepted, the app went ultimately unused due to disagreements between teachers on strategy going forwards.\
Even partial payment was refused despite a 1-hour presentation detailing the application's capabilities, mathematical implementations and coding style being explored in detail as proof of work.


{{ big_heading(name="Challenges") }}

---

The development of *Stellenwerttafel+* presented several distinct challenges:

*   Platform adaptation:
    *   Retain all required functionality while adapting the user experience to both Windows and Android environments.
    *   Consistent behaviour of math, especially floating points, were needed across all platforms.
*   New functionality to support for "base n" number systems.
*   External stakeholder
*   Time constraints:
    *   Completion of development was achieved within a highly compressed timeframe of a few days to address the supervising teachers deadline for a proof of concept to keeping costs low.
*   Dealing with nonpayment:
    *   Even though all requirements were met in record time, the school did not uphold their end of the bargain and did not provide any extra credit.
    *   In response the app was removed of all school devices and the source code was not turned over.


{{ big_heading(name="Features") }}

---

*Stellenwerttafel+* faithfully replicated and enhanced the learning methodology of the original "Stellenwerttafel" app.\
A particular focus was placed on a playful user experience for younger students, with the dots representing numbers sliding apart in a smooth and satisfying way when split up.

Key features included:

*   Interactive number learning:
    *   Students could intuitively manipulate numerical representations by dragging a "ball" that would visually fuse and split, thereby fostering an understanding of place value.
*   Comprehensive support for various number systems:
    *   Decimal (base 10)
    *   Binary (base 2)
    *   Hexadecimal (base 16)
    *   Octal (base 8)
    *   Custom "base n" systems.
*   Cross-platform availability:
    *   Windows
    *   Android


{{ big_heading(name="Availability") }}

---

For the aformentioned reasons *Stellenwerttafel+* was never formally released to the public or provided to the school.\
Ultimately, the extra credit was not required, and the school successfully secured funding for iPads, minimising impact on both sides.

Out of respect for the original author, Ulrich Kortenkamp, and due to the absence of explicit permission for distribution, *Stellenwerttafel+* was never made publicly available. The original source code is preserved, though there are no current plans for its release or further development.

*   Project Documentation:
